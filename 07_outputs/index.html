<div class="textcontainer"> <p class="margin"> </p> <h3>Week 7: Outputs</h3> <h4>Assignment: MVP Final Project</h4> <p> My final project was to design an adaptive, smart-sensing sole that adjusted its shape based on force applied at different points along its surface, using Velostat sensors to measure pressure and MPU-6050 sensors to measure force and movement. </p> The goal for this week was to complete a minimum viable version of my final project. The steps taken were: <br> 1. CAD the bases of the insoles in Fusion and 3D print them <br> 2. Intubate the sole with copper wiring and Velostat <br> 3. Solder wires to the copper, and cover the sole with insulative material with the exception of the Velostat tape <br> 4. Cover the other side of the sole with copper wiring such that the Velostat was situated between the copper wiring. <br> 5. Connect the microcontroller and components<br> 6. Calibrate force sensors <br> 7. Cover final part in fabric and test calibration again. <br> 8. Connect parts to stepper motor drivers (as opposed to just LEDs like in demo circuit) to convert output to actuators that will eventually alter shape. <p class="margin"> </p> <div class="class\"> <img src="IMG_4368.PNG" alt="Early foot model"> </div> <p class="caption"></p>
